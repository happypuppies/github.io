Headwater Streams Mapping in the Rice Lake Plains and Lake Iroquois Plain

Photo Credit: Chelsea Marcantonio via the Nature Conservancy of Canada

Abstract

Headwater streams are critically important to the conservation efforts of the Nature Conservancy of Canada (NCC) in the Rice Lake Plains and Lake Iroquois Plain area and consist of ephemeral and intermittent streams as well as streams with permanent surface flow. The purpose of this study was to create and visualize a network of all the headwater streams in this region. This study created hydrological models of the region’s watercourses and used these to map headwater areas for the NCC in both static and dynamic maps to aid in their conservation work. Enhanced Flow Direction Grid data was used to build a stream network attributed by Strahler stream order using a model constructed in ArcGIS Pro 2.7 with Arc Hydro tools. To visualize the depth of detailed information about surface runoff flow direction required by the NCC, a stream threshold value of 150 was used, which captures areas where runoff can accumulate even if there is no permanent surface water present. This stream network was used to create a series of static maps to be used primarily for print, but with versions colour-adjusted for digital use as well. Tools were constructed in ArcGIS Pro 2.7 to be used with the map data on a desktop utilizing ArcGIS Pro that allow a user to place a point at either the start of a headwater, or an outflow point on a major stream, and the tools will select either all connected downstream, or upstream segments respectively. A dynamic map was constructed using ArcGIS Online Experience Builder to visualize stream segments by Strahler stream order as well as by segments of Cold Water within the entire region and by specific focal areas defined by the NCC. Though all of these deliverables were of high quality and sufficient for the needs of this study, it is recommended that this study be repeated with higher-resolution base data when such data becomes available for the region.

Client: Nature Conservancy of Canada

The Nature Conservancy of Canada (NCC) is a non-profit conservation organization which operates across Canada to protect and restore significant ecosystems at risk, one being the Rice Lake Plains - Lake Iroquois Plain Natural Area. They also work directly with landowners who seek their help to provide guidance on protecting their own land which fall within these important habitats.

The Study Area

Location of the Rice Lake Plains – Lake Iroquois Plain Natural Area in south-central Ontario. The study area is located along the shoreline of Lake Ontario between the Greater Toronto Area and Belleville

Headwater streams, which feed larger drainage features, are vital parts of a watershed system, making up to 70 to 80 percent of a watershed’s total catchment area. Despite this, headwater streams are most at risk of destruction and alteration due to their small size and a lack of public knowledge about their importance. Any alteration to headwater streams can cause large disruptions to water quality and quantity in downstream habitats, as up to 90 percent of the flow in a river may originate from headwater streams.
